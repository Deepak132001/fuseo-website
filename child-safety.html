Child Safety Standards

Last Updated: December 20, 2024

Our Commitment to Child Safety

Fuseo is a professional networking platform designed exclusively for adults aged 18 and over. We have zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) and child sexual abuse material (CSAM).

We are committed to:

  • Preventing minors from accessing our platform
  • Detecting and removing any child sexual abuse material (CSAM)
  • Reporting suspected child exploitation to appropriate authorities
  • Cooperating fully with law enforcement investigations
  • Continuously improving our safety measures and technologies

Age Requirement: Fuseo is strictly for users 18 years of age and older. Anyone under 18 is prohibited from creating an account or using our services.

Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) Prevention

1. Age Verification

We enforce our 18+ age requirement through:

  • Mandatory date of birth declaration during registration
  • Account rejection for users under 18
  • Ongoing monitoring for potential underage users
  • Immediate account termination if underage use is detected

2. Content Moderation

We actively monitor and moderate content to prevent CSAM:

  • Automated detection systems for inappropriate content
  • Human review of flagged content
  • Photo upload screening before acceptance
  • Chat message monitoring for suspicious activity
  • Immediate removal of prohibited content

3. User Reporting System

Users can report concerns directly within the app:

  • In-app report button on all profiles and messages
  • Dedicated reporting category for child safety concerns
  • Anonymous reporting option available
  • 24-48 hour response time for safety reports
  • Immediate escalation of CSAM reports to our safety team

4. Technology and Detection

We employ technology to detect and prevent abuse:

  • Image hash matching against known CSAM databases
  • Pattern recognition for suspicious behavior
  • Regular security audits and updates
  • Encryption and secure data handling

Reporting to Authorities

National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC)

Fuseo is committed to reporting suspected CSAM to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) CyberTipline as required by law:

  • Immediate reporting of apparent CSAM
  • Preservation of evidence for law enforcement
  • Full cooperation with NCMEC investigations
  • Compliance with 18 U.S.C. ¬ß 2258A reporting requirements

NCMEC CyberTipline: All suspected CSAM is reported to NCMEC at www.cybertipline.org

Law Enforcement Cooperation

We work closely with law enforcement:

  • Response to legal requests within required timeframes
  • Preservation of account data for investigations
  • Cooperation with international authorities
  • Compliance with local, national, and international laws

Regional and National Authorities

In addition to NCMEC, we report to relevant authorities based on jurisdiction:

  • European Union: EUROPOL and national cybercrime units
  • United Kingdom: National Crime Agency (NCA) and Internet Watch Foundation (IWF)
  • Italy: Polizia Postale e delle Comunicazioni
  • Other regions: Local law enforcement and child protection agencies

User Safety Features

In-App Reporting

Users can report safety concerns directly:

  • Profile Reports: Report suspicious profiles or underage users
  • Message Reports: Report inappropriate messages or content
  • Photo Reports: Report concerning images
  • Block Function: Immediately block and report problematic users

Account Termination

We permanently ban accounts that:

  • Belong to users under 18
  • Share or solicit CSAM
  • Attempt to contact minors
  • Violate child safety policies
  • Engage in grooming behavior

Privacy and Data Protection

While protecting children is our priority, we also respect user privacy:

  • Reports are handled confidentially
  • Evidence preservation follows legal requirements
  • Data sharing limited to authorized authorities
  • GDPR and privacy law compliance

For more information, see our Privacy Policy.

Education and Awareness

We educate our community about child safety:

  • Clear community guidelines prohibiting illegal content
  • Age requirement notices throughout the app
  • Safety tips in our support documentation
  • Regular updates to safety policies

Continuous Improvement

We continuously improve our safety measures:

  • Regular safety audits and assessments
  • Technology updates to improve detection
  • Staff training on child safety protocols
  • Review of industry best practices
  • Collaboration with child safety organizations

  • Subject to legal action and prosecution
